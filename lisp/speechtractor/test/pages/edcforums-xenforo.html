    I sort of got into cowboy boots and hats a few years back. My boots with the more pointy toes and higher step heels don't get so much wear these days (a consequence of diabetes), but my ropers are still great. Mine are pull on; made by Justin. The wider toe and less dramatic heel make them super comfortable.

    If you are anywhere close to any stores that carry a selection of cowboy boots, talk to them. Some of the manufacturers do 'trunk shows' wherein they make the rounds of their better retailers. When a manufacturer is in your area you can be measured up for what will be semi-custom boots often at an attractive price. Or I guess you could contact some of the manufacturers to see if they will be doing such a 'circuit'.

    Cowboy up

    My first pair of cowboy boots was a pair of python skin Laredos that I found at Goodwill for $30. They are so comfortable they don't feel like shoes - they feel like my feet. The smooth leather soles made me slip too much, though, so I had a shoe repair guy put lug soles on them. They're perfect now, but snakeskin doesn't wear well when you beat the hell out of it, so I only wear them sometimes.

    I have a pair of black walking sole Ariat cowboy boots that are comfortable and that I don't have to worry about wearing in the rain and the mud and the bush.

    I've also got a pair of oxblood Luccheses with aftermarket rubber soles that look great despite being worn in very well.

    And a pair of steel toe Ariat workhogs, which are more like western style work boots than cowboy boots, but they're very solid and comfortable.

    I don't ride horses or anything, I just like boots.

    Growing up I wore Red Wing Pecos boots throughout my school days and through college. I don't wear them since they changed them up and ditched the cork sole.

    These days I like crepe soled cowboy boots for comfort. I have some Justin Tecko Crepe boots in deerskin, ostrich and elephant, some calfskin crepe boots from Lucchese & Twisted X, and buffalo hide boots from Chippewa & Twisted X. I've had other crepe soled boots from Ariat and Tony Lama that I liked as well. I had some custom crepe soled boots from 2 different makers but sold them as they didn't feel as good as the production boots I had.

    The only cowboy boots I wear for work including cattle ranching are lacers for the support. We round up cattle with a truck horn and repair fences from a truck or UTV so have no need for horses but I still like the style. My all time favorite is the discontinued Danner Longhorn Gore-Tex lace up ropers. I snag them on ebay whenever I see my size and have a couple of spares. Second favorite are the Ariat Hermosa boots although the last pair I had I got rid of because of a toe rub issue.

    My nicest looking pair of boots are some Lucchese elephant bark square toe boots with thick leather soles and goatskin shafts.

There was a exposed seam that pressed into my heel.The boots were supposed to be round nose as shown in the photo on the web site. The boots were very pointed toes. Not like the photo, also, the distressed leather as advertised was as thin as a paper towel. The finish looked fake and had a clear finish that was so hard it was like plastic. I was very disappointed. Now it will take close to a month to get a credit, that's another story with this national western wear company. Again, a very disappointing experience. I will continue to look and will probably settle on Ariat. The only thing I struggle with is the Ariat rubber heel and sole. Is the rubber better that leather. I look forward to the input from experienced boot wearers.
